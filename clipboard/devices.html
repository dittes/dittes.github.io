Where is my Clipboard?

One of the most common questions we get is regarding the clipboard location: "where is it on my specific device?" Below is a comprehensive guide to finding and managing your copied text across all major operating systems.

Jump to your device:
  • iPhone & iPad
  • Android & Samsung
  • Windows 11 & 10
  • Mac OS
  • Chromebook

How to find the Clipboard on iPhone

If you're wondering how to access the clipboard on my phone while using iOS, the reality is that Apple keeps it largely invisible. The clipboard iPhone system only holds one item at a time. To see what's on it, open the Notes app, tap an empty space, and select "Paste". To view a history of copied items, you must use a third-party app or a browser tool like our Online App.

Accessing Clipboard on Android & Samsung

The clipboard Android ecosystem is much more robust. If you are looking for the clipboard on Samsung devices, open your Samsung Keyboard, tap the three dots (menu icon), and select "Clipboard". You will see a visual history of texts and images you've copied recently. Gboard (Google Keyboard) users have a very similar clipboard icon built right into the top row of the keyboard.

Clipboard in Windows 11

Microsoft introduced an excellent built-in manager a few years ago. To open the clipboard Windows 11 (or Windows 10) interface, simply press the Windows Key + V. The first time you do this, you will be prompted to turn on Clipboard History. Once activated, it saves multiple copied items, allowing you to pin favorites and paste them anywhere.

Viewing Clipboard History on Mac

To view your current clipboard on macOS, open Finder, click "Edit" in the top menu bar, and select "Show Clipboard". However, native macOS does not support a running clipboard history Mac feature out of the box. You will need to rely on third-party utilities (like Maccy or Raycast) or a web-based clipboard hub to keep track of multiple copied strings.

Clipboard on Chromebook

Finding the clipboard Chromebook interface is incredibly simple. Just press the Search/Launcher key + V. This brings up an on-screen menu displaying the last five items you copied, including text and images. Click any item to instantly paste it into your active document.
